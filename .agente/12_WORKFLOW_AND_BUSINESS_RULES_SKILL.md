# 12_WORKFLOW_AND_BUSINESS_RULES_SKILL

## Objective

This skill defines the operational workflows, business rules, process states, approval flows and execution standards for the ERP ecosystem.

The objective is to maintain:

* predictable workflows
* safe business operations
* scalable operational logic
* controlled state transitions
* enterprise-grade process organization
* AI-understandable business behavior

The ERP must preserve operational consistency during continuous evolution.

---

## Core Workflow Principles

Always prioritize:

* predictable flows
* business rule integrity
* controlled transitions
* operational traceability
* modular workflow structure
* approval safety

Never alter workflows without explicit authorization.

---

## Business Rule Protection Rules

Business rules must remain centralized and protected.

Never:

* bypass operational validations
* skip required approvals
* alter operational sequences silently
* remove critical validations
* break process dependencies

All business operations must remain controlled.

---

## Workflow Structure Rules

Every operational process must define:

* start state
* transition rules
* approval requirements
* completion state
* rollback behavior
* error behavior

Workflows must remain predictable and traceable.

---

## State Transition Rules

All workflows must maintain controlled states.

Example:

```txt id="y2m8q4"
PENDING
APPROVED
REJECTED
COMPLETED
CANCELLED
```

Avoid uncontrolled state mutations.

---

## Approval Flow Rules

Approval processes must remain explicit.

Always define:

* who can approve
* approval hierarchy
* approval conditions
* rejection behavior
* audit tracking

Critical actions must never bypass approval logic.

---

## Operational Validation Rules

All workflows must validate:

* permissions
* required data
* operational conditions
* dependencies
* process integrity

Never allow invalid transitions.

---

## Workflow Isolation Rules

Each ERP module must maintain isolated workflow logic.

Examples:

```txt id="v8q1m6"
RH workflows
ESTOQUE workflows
FINANCEIRO workflows
LOGISTICA workflows
```

Avoid chaotic cross-module workflow logic.

---

## Automation Rules

Automations must remain:

* traceable
* predictable
* reversible when necessary
* isolated
* documented

Avoid hidden operational automations.

---

## Notification Rules

Workflow events may trigger:

* notifications
* logs
* approvals
* alerts
* audit records

Workflow communication must remain predictable.

---

## Auditability Rules

Critical operations must always maintain:

* action history
* timestamps
* responsible users
* state transitions
* approval tracking

ERP operations must remain auditable.

---

## Rollback Rules

Critical workflows should support rollback when possible.

Always define:

* rollback conditions
* rollback permissions
* rollback safety rules

Avoid irreversible critical operations without validation.

---

## Dependency Rules

Workflow dependencies must remain explicit.

Always expose:

* module relationships
* required approvals
* integration dependencies
* operational dependencies

Avoid hidden business logic dependencies.

---

## Workflow Documentation Rules

Always maintain updated:

* WORKFLOW_MAP.md
* BUSINESS_RULES.md
* APPROVAL_FLOWS.md
* DATA_FLOW_MAP.md

Operational documentation must remain synchronized.

---

## Scalability Rules

Workflow architecture must support:

* future modules
* future automations
* enterprise growth
* larger operational complexity
* future AI automation

Never create temporary workflow structures.

---

## Architecture Preservation Rules

Workflow implementations must:

* respect ERP modularization
* preserve architecture consistency
* avoid hidden dependencies
* remain maintainable

Operational growth must never compromise architecture quality.

---

## Final Objective

Transform the ERP into:

* a scalable operational ecosystem
* a predictable business platform
* an AI-understandable workflow architecture
* a maintainable enterprise process system
* a safe long-term operational structure

The ERP workflows must remain organized, traceable and scalable during continuous evolution.
